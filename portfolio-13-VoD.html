VoD Platform

Overview

The Client, based in Dubai, commissioned me to review and redesign the initial prototype of the Vod Platform.
My role was to evaluate usability, visual clarity, and interaction flow, and to propose a unified home screen concept that could scale seamlessly across TV, mobile, tablet, and in-car displays.

The Problem

The first Vod Home prototype showcased rich functionality but presented significant usability hurdles. Navigation was fragmented, forcing users to zig-zag across three axes (icon belt, tab row, horizontal carousels).

Key issues included:

  • Complex interaction flow → high click/tap counts for basic tasks.
  • Legibility challenges → light text over artwork with poor contrast.
  • Weak focus indicators → difficult for TV viewers sitting three meters away.
  • Unnecessary entry gates → a “Whoâ€™s watching?” welcome screen added friction before reaching content.

The result was a tiring, error-prone experience that risked alienating casual viewers, older users, and families.

Hypothesis

If navigation were reduced to two clear axes (vertical + horizontal), supported by strong accessibility patterns and a scalable component system, the platform could:

  • Cut down interaction steps by ~40%.
  • Improve legibility and focus recognition for all users.
  • Ensure cross-device cohesion with a single design language.
  • Surface valuable content faster, increasing engagement.

Role & Responsibilities

  • User Research
    • Evaluated the prototype through a heuristic review.
    • Identified accessibility gaps against WCAG standards.
    • Mapped common click-paths to quantify interaction cost.
  • User Interface Design
    • Introduced a vertical rail for primary navigation.
    • Added a lightweight horizontal tab bar for contextual filtering.
    • Reorganized utilities (search, quick apps, account, AI assistant) into a top-right cluster.
    • Removed the welcome screen, landing directly on Continue Watching for faster value delivery.
    • Developed a box-first component grid scalable to TV, mobile, and car displays.

Output

The redesigned system delivered:

  • Figma mid-fidelity mockups with adaptive layouts across devices.
  • Accessibility improvements: WCAG AA compliant contrast, persistent focus states, and color-blind safe iconography.
  • Interaction efficiency: a ~40% reduction in remote clicks on common journeys.
